About Us

Dighe Kulkarni Org Advisors is a specialist consulting firm focused on Organisation Design — helping companies unlock performance by aligning structure, talent, and execution with strategy.

We were founded on a clear belief: When organisations are designed well, people thrive, decisions move faster, and businesses grow with purpose.

Founded by Amruta Dighe Kulkarni, former Talent & Organisation Consulting Director at Accenture, the firm brings over 20+ years of experience in large-scale transformation, people-centered design, and leadership capability building. With deep consulting roots across India and Singapore, we translate strategic intent into organisation models that work — structurally, culturally, and operationally.

We work with promoters, CXOs, HR leaders, and investors across Indian and global conglomerates, family-run businesses, mid-sized growth firms, and scaling start-ups — spanning FMCG, Retail, BFSI, Healthcare, Tech, and the Public Sector.

Frequently Asked Questions

Isn't Org Design just about redrawing the org chart?

No. The org chart is only one piece. Real design aligns five critical levers — structure, processes, people, rewards, and culture — with your business strategy. This alignment determines whether your teams can move fast, work across silos, and deliver results.

How is Organisation Design different from Corporate Strategy, HR Strategy, and Organisation Development?

Each plays a distinct role:

  • Corporate Strategy defines where the business is going.
  • Organisation Design shapes how it will get there — through roles, decision rights, and collaboration models.
  • HR Strategy ensures you have the right talent and workforce to deliver.
  • Organisation Development focuses on mindset, behaviour, and cultural change.

Think of it this way: Strategy sets the direction. Design builds the engine. HR powers the people. Development shapes the culture.

At Dighe Kulkarni Org Advisors, we work at the intersection of all these elements. While our core focus is Organisation Design, we partner with a trusted network of experts to support clients in areas such as Strategy Planning, HR, and OD — ensuring that change is not only well-designed, but also well-executed and sustained.

Why engage an external partner? Can't our HR or Strategy team lead this in-house?

Internal teams bring context and commitment. But a dedicated partner brings structure, objectivity, and a neutral voice — essential for challenging assumptions, surfacing misalignments, and driving cross-leadership alignment. It ensures momentum without overloading internal teams.

Why invest in organisation design now especially if we're already doing well?

What works today may not scale tomorrow. As companies grow, execution often slows due to unclear roles, legacy processes, or bottlenecks. Design helps pre-empt those issues — enabling agility, reducing dependence on a few individuals, and building execution muscle for the future.

Isn't hiring better talent enough? Why do we need to redesign the organisation?

Great talent needs the right environment to thrive. Without clear roles, decision rights, and collaboration models, even top performers struggle. Org Design helps convert talent potential into sustained impact — a critical gap in many firms investing in leadership and HiPo programs.

We're a mid-sized or family-run company. Is org design relevant for us?

Absolutely. For growing, founder-led firms, org design helps shift from intuition-led decisions to scalable, professionalised models — critical for growth, succession, and long-term sustainability.

What's the ROI of investing in Organisation Design?
  • Faster decisions
  • Clearer ownership
  • Better cross-team collaboration
  • Stronger leadership focus
  • Higher talent retention and engagement

For companies scaling quickly, it’s a strategic investment that improves both performance and resilience.

Is it worth the cost? How do we know it worked?

The hidden cost of poor design — attrition, rework, stalled decisions — is often much higher. Outcomes are visible through:

  • Shorter cycle times
  • Increased team effectiveness
  • Higher leadership bandwidth
  • Tangible business results
